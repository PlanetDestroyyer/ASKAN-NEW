Frequently Asked Questions

How does hydroponics work?

Hydroponics is a method of growing plants without using soil. Instead, plants are grown in a nutrient-rich water solution, promoting rapid growth.

What kind of plants can be grown using hydroponics?

Virtually any type of plant can be grown using hydroponics, including vegetables, fruits, herbs, and flowers. Examples include cabbage, capsicum, brinjal, tomato, and spinach.

What value pH should be maintained in a hydroponic system?

The ideal pH range for a hydroponic system is 5.5 to 6.5, although specific requirements may vary slightly depending on the plant species.

Why is pH important in hydroponics?

pH plays a crucial role in hydroponics as it directly affects nutrient availability to plants. Maintaining the correct pH level ensures optimal nutrient uptake and healthy plant growth.

What causes plants to die in a hydroponic system?

Common reasons for plant death in hydroponic systems include lack of water, improper pH levels, incorrect fertilizer usage, pest infestations, and environmental stressors.

Is hydroponic farming a new technology?

No, hydroponic farming has ancient roots, dating back to civilizations such as the Hanging Gardens of Babylon in the 6th century B.C. and practices by the Egyptians.

Is hydroponic farming expensive?

Hydroponic farming can be cost-effective, with options for DIY setups and gradual expansion. It doesn't have to be expensive.

Is hydroponic farming harmful for nature?

No, hydroponic farming is considered environmentally friendly, minimizing land, water, and pesticide usage compared to conventional agriculture.
